Kang Daeun Kang Daeun






Daeun Kang is Born in Seoul, Korea
Currently residing in Michigan, US

Growing up under frequent relocation, diasporic experience has become central to Kang’s practice. She explores varying means and modes of human presence through the contextual relationship between matter and form; manifesting using sawdust, processed wood, and tree trunks. Her current sculptures are simultaneously deconstructive and constructive, creating overlapping positive and negative forms whose tactility challenges the sculptural language and expression under Western hegemony. Her work is born out of her interests in womanist theory, post-colonial narratives, and aesthetic theory.
