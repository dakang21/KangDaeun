Phidia Kang(Kahng)is a sculptor who grew up in rural Korea and the Southern United States. Coming from diasporic communities, Kang is interested in the tension inherent in selfhood; in the ways we negotiate and structure trust, belief, and boundaries. Primarily working with wood fibers and casting methods, her practice revolves around a material ecosystem built through her repetitive making process, often resulting in appendage-like studio artifacts that explore material translation and sculptural tension.
