Phidia Kang (Kahng) is a womanist sculptor who was raised in rural Korea and the Southern United States. Coming from diasporic communities, Kang explores the inherent tension in selfhood, particularly focusing on the plasticity of our existence. Her practice primarily involves working with wood fibers and casting methods, facilitating a material ecosystem through her creative process. This often yields studio artifacts resembling appendages, which delve into transversality and tactility within the realm of sculpture.
