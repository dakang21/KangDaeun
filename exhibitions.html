PAAI (Practice for Inter-Asia Art Initiative) Reimagining Asian Aesthetics Through Contemporary Art April 23 – May 8, 2025
Boîte, Seoul
written by artguide.team@gmail.com
photo by ArtGuide Team / Lee Hyunseok
poster design by Borui Zhang
PAAI is an artist-led research collective that explores Asian aesthetics by bridging ancient philosophies and contemporary practices.
This exhibition brings together six artists—Phidia Kang, Kim Hyunho, Na Sun, Park Seongsu, Lee Younjin, and Lim Jihyun— who delve into themes such as Womanist art history, East Asian cosmology, embodiment in painting theory, hybridity, and spiritual time. Through their work, they reconnect fragmented pasts with the present and future.

Clouded: Korean Diaspora Aug 18 - Nov 10, 2017
Mason Fine Art Gallery
415 Plaster Ave NE, Atlanta, GA, USA
Living in a world where we are all simultaneously connected and disconnected from each other... (중략) ... “Clouded” is a reference to traditional Korean folklore, where clouds were the embodiment of all things ideal; symbolic of enlightenment. This is what Clouded seeks to address. As we become rapidly more and more connected, there is a sense of awkwardness and disconnection that comes from this mutation of ideas and cultures, and from a lack of physical interaction.
